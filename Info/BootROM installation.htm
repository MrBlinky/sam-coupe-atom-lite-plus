Installing the AL+ BootROM

 

The AL+ BootROM is a replacement ROM for the SAM Coupé that will make the SAM start up faster than usual and boots directly from a Compact Flash Card in Any AL+ interface from any CF-Slot. To replace the ROM in your SAM Coupé you need to open your SAM. There are no worries for breaking any warranties on your SAM anymore. But care should be taken not to damage the fragile keyboard leads. Please read the instructions below carefully.

 

Opening the SAM Coupé

 

Open the SAM Coupe by turning it upside down and then unscrew all the screws on the bottom. But not those on the MGT  serial number sticker. Use a small philips screwdriver. Do not use a  screwdriver that is too small or too big otherwise you may damage the screws. If you have any disk drives in your SAM then remove these before you turn your SAM around. You don’t want them to slide out while you turn your SAM around.

 

Lift the cover of the SAM by one or two inches by holding it at the left and right side then move the cover about half an inch backwards and put the cover down.Now raise the cover  just at the front by two or three inches so you can look into the opening. You will see two leads coming from the keyboard in the centre that are connected to SAM’s circuitboard. These need to be pulled out of their sockets with care.

 

Keep the cover steady with one hand and use your free hand to slide through the opening. Do not lift the cover higher then 3 or 4 inches or you may put too much strain on the keyboard leads. Remove the lead closest to the front first. Put  your index finger behind the lead in the middle and your thumb in the front hold the lead firmly and pull it out in a vertical motion. Do not bend or twist the lead.

 

With the front lead removed you can raise the cover a bit further so you can reach to other lead and pull it out the same way. When both leads are pulled from their sockets you can take the cover off. With the cover removed you can have a good look at the T shaped circuit board. In the middle is a square chip with the text VLSI. That’s the is the ASIC. Right next to it at the right is the ROM you have to replace

 

Replacing the ROM

 

The SAM ROM can be removed from its socket using a IC puller or a small flat screwdriver. Use the flat screwdriver as a wrench.Lift the rom a little at each side and continue doing this until the ROM is completely out of its socket. If the capacitor at the right of the ROM is blocking the way then you can gently bend it towards the circuit board to get better access.After the ROM is removed you can push the AL+BootROM into the ROM socket. Make sure that the small notch on one side of the Boot ROM is pointing towards the ASIC .

 

With the  AL+ Boot ROM fitted connect power and the TV or Monitor cables and power up to check the ROM works  correctly. You should see a familiar rainbow screen with the boot ROM version number. When you have  you can put SAM back together following the earlier steps in reverse order. When at the point of  reconnecting the keyboard leads you may decide to connect power and the TV or monitor cables again and check that all the keys function properly. When the keyboard is OK you can remove the power and monitor cables again and continue assembling.
